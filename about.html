About

Hi, I'm Minaya — a global marketer, ex-entrepreneur, and lifelong curious soul.

I'm a high-performer with a soul. A digital marketer, global strategist, and ex-entrepreneur who's happiest helping impact-first businesses grow — and no, I don't mean the generic mission-vision stuff. I mean businesses that actually give a crap about the world and the people in it.

I've spent 8+ years building a career in the startup ecosystem across three continents — from navigating the high-speed chaos of a Series E Silicon Valley startup in Berlin, to co-founding a yoga retreat venture in Spain, to scaling an eating disorder recovery platform in the US. Lately, I've been leading D2C lifecycle strategy, marketing ops, and AI adoption at a global mobility brand.

My toolkit lives at the intersection of growth strategy, CRM & lifecycle marketing, funnel optimization, workflow automation, and AI integration. But what really drives me? Impact. A team that's all in. Building something worth caring about.

I'm organized and tenacious (Capricorn energy duh), but I also lead with empathy and warmth. I love digging into data as much as I love understanding the human behind it. When things get messy, I don't quit — I get curious.

Manifesto

What I stand for, what I'm done with.

What I stand for

  • Ownership & curiosity. Everything is figureoutable. Own your sh*t. There's always a way if there's a will.
  • Work that feels alive. I want to build things I'm genuinely excited to talk about.
  • Human-centered AI. Not building systems that produce more just because we can. AI isn't about doing more — it's about doing better.
  • Radical authenticity. I bring my whole self to work. No "work persona".

What I'm done with

  • Burnout culture. Vanity metrics aren't worth my sanity.
  • Performative hours. If I can finish a task in two hours, I'm not staying until eight for appearances. Results > clock-ins.
  • The "work persona". If I wanted to spend my day acting like someone else, I'd be in the theater.
  • Mediocrity. Life is messy, marketing is hard, and excuses are for losers.
